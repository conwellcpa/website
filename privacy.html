Privacy Policy

How we handle the information you share with us through this website.

Last updated: July 2026

Christopher Conwell, CPA PC ("we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy. This policy explains what information we collect through this website, how we use it, and the choices you have. It applies to this website only, not to the separate, confidential handling of client information under an engagement, which is governed by our professional obligations and your engagement agreement.

Information we collect

  • Information you provide. When you submit our contact form, we collect the details you enter — such as your name, email address, phone number, and the contents of your message.
  • Information collected automatically. Like most websites, our host may record standard technical data such as IP address, browser type, and pages visited, for security and to keep the site running.
  • Analytics (if enabled). We may use a privacy-conscious analytics tool (such as Google Analytics) to understand, in aggregate, how visitors use the site. This may involve cookies or similar technologies.

How we use your information

  • To respond to your inquiry and communicate with you about a possible engagement.
  • To operate, secure, and improve this website.
  • To comply with legal or regulatory obligations.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not send marketing email to people who contact us unless they ask us to.

How your information is shared

We share information only as needed to run the site and respond to you — for example, with the service providers that host the site and process form submissions (such as Netlify) — and when required by law. These providers handle the data on our behalf under their own privacy and security terms.

Data security

We take reasonable measures to protect the information submitted through this site, including serving the site over HTTPS. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security. Please do not send sensitive documents or tax information through the website contact form; once you are a client, we provide a secure portal for that.

Your choices

  • You can choose not to submit the contact form; you can always reach us by phone or email instead.
  • You can set your browser to refuse cookies, though some features may not work as intended.
  • You can ask us what information we hold about you, or ask us to delete it, by contacting us using the details below.

Third-party links

This site may link to third-party websites (for example, a map or scheduling service). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites; their policies govern your use of them.

Children's privacy

This website is intended for business owners and adults. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be reflected on this page.
