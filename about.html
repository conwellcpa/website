The Practice

Why this firm works the way it does

Most accounting relationships break down in the same place: the planning. The return gets filed competently, but no one is steering the decisions that determine what's on it. By the time anyone looks, the year is closed.

Christopher Conwell, CPA PC was built to close that gap. Christopher holds a Master of Science in Taxation and a Bachelor of Science in Accounting, both from SUNY Old Westbury — a graduate specialization in tax, not a general accounting background — and has spent more than a decade advising business owners and helping them keep significantly more of what they earn.

The practice is kept deliberately small so every client gets genuine attention and direct access, and so the advice is grounded in a real understanding of their situation, not a template.

The result is a different kind of relationship: a CPA who knows your structure, anticipates the decisions ahead, and is reachable when one of them lands on your desk.

How We Operate

Principles that hold under pressure

Judgment over volume

We'd rather serve fewer clients well than many superficially. Depth is the whole value, and depth doesn't scale infinitely.

Candor over comfort

You'll get our honest read, including when it isn't what you hoped to hear. Good advice is worth more than agreeable advice.

Proactive by default

The valuable work happens before deadlines, not after. We're built to anticipate, not to react in April.
